Franklin Delano Roosevelt

Arguably the Greatest U.S. President of all Time

Notable Accomplishments

  • Implemented the "New Deal", a program designed to end the Great Depression
  • Led the country during WWII as one of the primary figures of the Allied Powers
  • Repealed the 18th Amendment which prohibited the sale of alcohol
  • Implemented numerous new federal agencies such as the Securities and Exchange Commission, National Labor Relactions Act, Social Security, and the Fair Labor Standards Act
  • Created the "March of Dimes" program to fund polio research which resulted in a cure

Memorable Quotes

  • "The only thing we have to fear is fear itself." March 4, 1933
  • "I see one-third of a nation ill-housed, ill-clad, ill-nourished." "The test of our progress is not whether we add more to the abundance of those who have much; it is whether we provide enough for those who have too little." January 20, 1937
  • "We must scrupulously guard the civil rights and civil liberties of all our citizens, whatever their background. We must remember that any oppression, any injustice, any hatred, is a wedge designed to attack our civilization." January 9, 1940
